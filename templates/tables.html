How performance was measured?

For regression models such as Linear Regression, Random Forest Regressor, k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), and XGBoost Regressor, there are several performance metrics you can use to evaluate their effectiveness. Here are some common regression metrics:
  1. Mean Absolute Error (MAE) : MAE represents the average absolute difference between the actual and predicted values. It provides a measure of the average magnitude of errors, without considering their direction.


  2. Mean Squared Error (MSE) : MSE is similar to MAE but squares the differences between actual and predicted values. Squaring emphasizes larger errors, making it sensitive to outliers. MSE is widely used and has desirable mathematical properties.


  3. Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) : RMSE is the square root of the MSE and is in the same unit as the dependent variable. It provides a measure of the average magnitude of errors in the same unit as the target variable.


  4. R-squared (R2) : R2 represents the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variables. It ranges from 0 to 1, with 1 indicating a perfect fit. R2 is a measure of goodness of fit and is often used to assess how well the model explains the variability in the data.
